# Support Ticket #006

**Status:** Resolved
**Date opened:** 2024-09-22
**Date closed:** 2024-09-30
**Product:** Nova Phone X1
**Category:** Trade-in / Payments

## Customer Issue
Customer traded in an iPhone 12 for a quoted **$210** in store credit, but after inspection the final offer was reduced to **$95** citing a "non-original screen replacement." The customer disputed this, stating they had only ever used Apple-authorized service centers.

## Diagnosis
Trade-in inspection team flagged signs of a non-OEM display assembly. Customer provided Apple service records via Apple Support showing official screen repair on 2024-02 — which used a genuine part but a refurbished one (Apple's own service program does this routinely).

## Resolution
Trade-in team revised the offer back to the original **$210** after reviewing the Apple service records. Customer accepted and applied the credit toward a new Nova Phone X1 order.

Issued a goodwill **$30 store credit** for the inconvenience and 8-day delay.

## Notes
Update trade-in inspector training: Apple's own refurbished service parts are still considered OEM and should not trigger the "non-original" downgrade. Add a Q&A entry to the internal inspection guide.
